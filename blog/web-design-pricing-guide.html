Web Design Pricing: A Realistic Guide to What Things Actually Cost

Web design pricing spans an enormous range — from a few hundred dollars for a simple template-based site to well into six figures for a fully custom enterprise build — and understanding what actually drives that range helps set a realistic, appropriate budget.

DIY website builders (Squarespace, Wix): typically $200-$1,000 per year including hosting, requiring the business owner to do the actual design and setup work themselves.

Freelance designers: typically $1,000-$10,000 depending on scope, offering a middle ground between DIY affordability and agency-level customization and expertise.

Small to mid-size agencies: typically $5,000-$50,000, generally offering more comprehensive service, project management, and often broader capabilities beyond pure design.

Large or specialized agencies: $50,000 and up, often for complex, highly custom, or enterprise-scale projects requiring significant development and strategic work beyond visual design alone.

Number of unique page designs needed, complexity of custom functionality (beyond a standard content site), whether content (copywriting, photography) is included or handled separately, and the depth of strategic and UX work involved before design even begins.

Hosting, domain renewal, security maintenance, and future content updates are recurring costs that continue well beyond the initial project, often underestimated in initial budget planning but genuinely important to factor in from the start.

Freelancers often offer lower cost and more direct, personal communication, but represent more risk if that individual becomes unavailable or overcommitted. Agencies typically offer more redundancy, project management structure, and broader skill coverage, at a correspondingly higher price reflecting that additional overhead and capability.

Underestimating content creation costs (copywriting, photography) as a separate line item from the design and development work itself, and failing to budget for ongoing maintenance and hosting beyond the initial launch, are two of the most common, avoidable budget planning mistakes.

Match your budget expectations to the actual scope and provider type genuinely needed, rather than assuming the cheapest option available will deliver the same result as a more comprehensive engagement — a mismatch between budget and genuine project scope is one of the most common sources of disappointment in web design projects.

Web design pricing varies enormously based on provider type and project scope, ranging from a few hundred dollars for DIY builders to well into six figures for complex custom or enterprise builds. Setting a realistic budget means matching expectations to actual scope, and remembering to account for ongoing costs beyond the initial project launch.
