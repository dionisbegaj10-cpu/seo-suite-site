Local SEO for Dummies

Search "plumber" on a phone and Google shows a map with three businesses before any regular website results, that map block is called the Local Pack, and getting into it drives more calls for local businesses than almost anything else. It is a separate ranking system from normal SEO, based on proximity, relevance, and prominence, not just keywords on a page.

A complete, accurate Google Business Profile is the single biggest factor. That means the exact same business name, address, and phone number everywhere online, correct categories, real photos, and business hours kept up to date, since a profile with mismatched information across directories actively hurts rankings. Reviews come next, both the quantity and how recently they arrived, a business with 60 reviews from the last year consistently outranks one with 200 reviews from three years ago.

Citations, meaning the business listed consistently across directories like Yelp, the Better Business Bureau, and industry-specific sites, reinforce that the business is real and in the location it claims. None of this requires a large budget, claiming the Google Business Profile is free, asking happy customers for a review costs nothing, and these fundamentals typically move rankings faster than any other SEO tactic for a business that depends on local customers.

Google Posts, a free feature inside Google Business Profile, lets a business publish short updates, offers, or events that show up directly on the profile in search and maps results, and posting even a couple of times a month is a low-effort way to keep a profile looking active, which Google's local algorithm does factor into how prominent a listing appears.

Responding to every review, positive or negative, professionally and promptly, is a small habit that reinforces trust signals Google's local algorithm weighs, beyond just the star rating itself. None of these local ranking factors require a large team either, a business owner checking in weekly can keep a profile competitive without outside help.

Service-area businesses without a public storefront — plumbers, mobile groomers, home contractors — use a modified version of the standard local setup: hiding the exact address and defining a service area radius instead. This generally results in somewhat more limited visibility than a business with a genuine physical location customers can visit, which is worth knowing going in rather than being surprised by later.

Local link building deserves specific attention separate from citations: a link from the city's chamber of commerce, a local newspaper covering a community event, or a sponsorship of a local sports team carries real geographic relevance signal that a generic national directory link simply cannot replicate. These local relationships tend to be genuinely easier to build than competitive national backlinks, since local organizations are often actively looking for community partners.

Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between local SEO and regular SEO?

Local SEO specifically targets people searching in a geographic area near a business, relying heavily on signals like Google Business Profile, local citations, and reviews that regular SEO doesn't emphasize as much.

Does a business need a physical storefront for local SEO to matter?

No — service-area businesses (plumbers, contractors, mobile services) still benefit from local SEO, just with a slightly different setup than a business with a public storefront.
