The Local SEO Checklist: Everything Worth Getting Right

Local SEO involves several distinct components working together — here's a practical, prioritized checklist covering what actually matters most for most local businesses.

Fully complete and verify the profile, choose accurate primary and secondary categories, write a clear, genuinely descriptive business description, keep hours accurate including holiday adjustments, add quality photos regularly, and post updates consistently rather than treating the profile as a one-time setup.

Include a clear, accurate contact page with complete address and contact information, mention service areas clearly and honestly, ensure the site is genuinely mobile-friendly given how much local search happens on phones, and make sure the site loads quickly, since local searchers are often looking for immediate, actionable information.

Establish one exact, consistent format for business name, address, and phone number, audit existing citations and directories for accuracy against that format, and correct any inconsistencies found rather than letting them persist across the web.

Actively and consistently ask satisfied customers for reviews rather than hoping they happen organically, respond thoughtfully to all reviews received (positive and negative), and never offer incentives specifically in exchange for reviews, which violates most platforms' policies.

Build citations on genuinely relevant general and industry-specific directories, prioritizing quality and accuracy over indiscriminate volume, and use a data aggregator service where helpful to maintain consistency efficiently across many secondary listings.

Pursue genuine local backlink opportunities — local news coverage, community involvement, local business association memberships, partnerships with complementary local businesses — rather than purely transactional, low-quality local directory submissions.

Implement LocalBusiness (or a relevant industry-specific subtype) schema markup, ensuring it accurately reflects current business information, and verify correct implementation using Google's Rich Results Test tool.

Create dedicated, genuinely unique pages and separate Google Business Profiles per location rather than generic, near-duplicate templates, and maintain NAP consistency separately for each individual location.

Regularly check Google Business Profile insights for search terms and customer actions, monitor for new or duplicate listings that might need correction, and periodically re-audit citation accuracy, since local information tends to drift out of sync over time without active maintenance.

Strong local SEO comes from consistently executing across several interconnected areas — a complete, actively maintained Google Business Profile, accurate and consistent citations, genuine local backlinks, active review management, and supporting on-site signals — rather than any single tactic in isolation. Treating local SEO as ongoing maintenance, not a one-time setup, is what separates businesses that stay visible from those whose local presence gradually drifts out of sync.

Frequently asked questions

What's the single most important item on a local SEO checklist?

A fully completed, accurate Google Business Profile — it's usually the highest-impact, lowest-effort item, since it directly affects Maps visibility and the local search results panel.

How often should a local SEO checklist be revisited?

At least quarterly — business details, hours, and service areas change, and an outdated checklist item (like old hours during a holiday) can actively hurt the customer experience.
