How to Perform an SEO Audit

A solid audit starts with checking indexing status in Google Search Console, then moves to technical health, crawling the site to catch broken links, redirect chains, and duplicate titles, checking page speed and mobile experience directly on a phone, and confirming robots.txt and noindex tags are not accidentally blocking anything important. From there, reviewing site architecture and auditing existing content honestly against current search intent and accuracy rounds out the foundational work.

Evaluating keyword performance shows what pages actually rank for versus what they were intended to target, assessing the backlink profile reveals gaps against competitors, and checking AI and local visibility, where relevant, rounds out a full picture of where a site actually stands rather than where it was assumed to stand.

An audit that produces a hundred-item list with no priority order is not actually useful, findings should be sorted by impact and effort, fixing high-impact, low-effort items first for the fastest visible wins, then converting the prioritized list into concrete next steps with a date set to revisit and measure what actually improved.
